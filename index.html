Franchise Origins

From Chicago to Washington

The franchise that would become the Washington Wizards began its journey in 1961 as the Chicago Packers, one of the NBA's expansion teams. The team's early years were marked by frequent relocations and name changes, reflecting the growing pains of a young franchise finding its identity.

After just one season in Chicago, the team moved to Baltimore in 1963, becoming the Baltimore Bullets‚Äîa name with deep roots in the city's basketball history, borrowed from an earlier team that had won the 1948 BAA Championship.

The franchise finally found its permanent home in the Washington, D.C. metropolitan area in 1973, eventually settling into the Capital Centre in Landover, Maryland. In 1997, the team was renamed the Washington Wizards as part of a rebranding effort to distance itself from gun violence associations.

The Championship

NBA Champions

1978

Led by Finals MVP Wes Unseld and the dynamic scoring of Elvin Hayes, the Washington Bullets defeated the Seattle SuperSonics 4-3 in a thrilling seven-game series to capture the only NBA Championship in franchise history. The series featured incredible drama, with six of the seven games decided by single digits.
